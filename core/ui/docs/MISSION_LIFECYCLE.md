Version:

---

# Purpose

Every operation performed within Trident follows the same lifecycle.

The mission type may change.

The threat may change.

The tools may change.

The workflow does not.

Consistency creates quality.

Quality creates trust.

---

# Phase 1 — Receive Mission

Every operation begins with an objective.

Examples:

• Bug Bounty

• Penetration Test

• Incident Response

• CTF

• OSINT Investigation

• Threat Hunt

Output:

Mission Objective

---

# Phase 2 — Scope & Authorization

Confirm:

Authorization

Rules of Engagement

Scope

Restrictions

Safe Harbor

If authorization cannot be established:

Stop.

No Scope.

No Strike.

---

# Phase 3 — Mission Creation

Create:

Mission ID

Mission Folder

Initial Metadata

Mission Context

Mission Owner

The mission now becomes the source of truth.

---

# Phase 4 — Playbook Selection

Select one or more operational playbooks.

Examples:

Quick Recon

Bug Bounty

Identity Mapping

Secret Hunting

API Assessment

Cloud Assessment

Incident Response

---

# Phase 5 — Discovery

Observe.

Collect.

Document.

Do not assume.

Goal:

Reduce uncertainty.

---

# Phase 6 — Analysis

Interpret the evidence.

Identify patterns.

Build relationships.

Form hypotheses.

Never confuse evidence with conclusions.

---

# Phase 7 — Validation

Every finding must be verified.

Confirm:

Reproducibility

Impact

Scope

Confidence

False positives removed.

---

# Phase 8 — Evidence Collection

Capture everything required to reproduce the investigation.

Evidence should be:

Accurate

Timestamped

Organized

Repeatable

Evidence outlives memory.

---

# Phase 9 — Reporting

Communicate findings clearly.

Include:

Summary

Impact

Risk

Evidence

Remediation

Professional reporting is part of the mission.

---

# Phase 10 — Remediation

Translate findings into action.

Recommend:

Immediate fixes

Long-term improvements

Validation steps

Lessons for future missions

---

# Phase 11 — Lessons Learned

Every mission teaches.

Record:

Successes

Failures

Unexpected discoveries

Workflow improvements

Future ideas

The operator should improve after every mission.

---

# Phase 12 — Knowledge Graph Update

This phase makes Trident different.

Convert mission knowledge into structured intelligence.

Examples:

New Entity

New Relationship

Updated Observation

New Technique

Updated Confidence

Knowledge compounds.

---

# Phase 13 — Mission Archive

A mission is complete when:

Evidence is preserved.

Knowledge is updated.

Reports are complete.

Lessons are recorded.

The archive becomes available for future investigations.

Nothing valuable is forgotten.
