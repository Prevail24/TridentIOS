# 📢 Reporter

> **The communications layer of the Trident Intelligence Operating System.**

---

# Purpose

Reporter transforms intelligence into understanding.

Its responsibility is not investigation.

Its responsibility is communication.

Reporter ensures every audience receives the right information, at the right level of detail, in the right format.

---

# Philosophy

Raw intelligence is valuable.

Communicated intelligence is actionable.

The same Mission may require:

- an executive summary
- a technical report
- a SOC alert
- an investigation timeline
- a classroom explanation
- an HTB walkthrough

Reporter adapts communication without changing the underlying intelligence.

---

# Responsibilities

Reporter owns:

- Mission briefings
- Executive summaries
- Technical reports
- Timelines
- Visualizations
- Audience adaptation
- Communication formatting
- Delivery preparation

Reporter communicates intelligence.

It never creates it.

---

# Reporter Does NOT Own

Reporter intentionally avoids operational responsibilities.

It never owns:

- Mission execution
- Reasoning
- Evidence collection
- Memory
- Capability execution
- Scheduling

It communicates the Mission.

Nothing more.

---

# Explainability

Every briefing should answer:

- What happened?
- Why does it matter?
- What evidence supports it?
- How confident are we?
- What should happen next?

Understanding is the goal.

---

# Visual Communication

- Timelines
- Entity graphs
- Relationship diagrams
- Network maps
- Charts
- Tables
- Intelligence summaries

---

# Traceability

Every statement should be traceable.

Readers should always be able to determine:

- Supporting observations
- Supporting evidence
- Related findings
- Mission references

Communication should never lose provenance.

---

# Adaptation

Reporter should adapt:

- Technical depth
- Terminology
- Length
- Structure
- Format
- Tone

Without changing factual content.
