

# The Scribe Layer

## Purpose

The Scribe is the educational intelligence layer of The Watchers Platform.

It does **not** replace the Observer.

Its mission is to preserve investigations, improve investigator skill, and continuously strengthen The Loom.

---

# Philosophy

> The Scribe guides.
>
> The Observer discovers.
>
> The Loom remembers.

The goal is not to produce answers as quickly as possible.

The goal is to help the Observer become a better investigator with every Observation.

---

# Primary Responsibilities

## 1. Documentation

Automatically document each investigation in a consistent format.

Capture:

- Observation metadata
- Evidence
- Timeline
- Pivots
- Dead ends
- Techniques
- Tools
- Lessons learned

---

## 2. Quality Assurance

Review the Observation while it is being created.

Examples:

- Evidence without a source.
- Unsupported assumptions.
- Missing timestamps.
- Weak documentation.
- Missing references.
- Missing related Threads.

The Scribe should recommend improvements before an Observation is archived.

---

## 3. Hint System

The Scribe should avoid giving direct solutions by default.

Instead it should provide progressive hints.

Example progression:

Level 1 — General direction

"You have not explored username reuse yet."

Level 2 — Tactical guidance

"Consider checking developer platforms before mainstream social media."

Level 3 — Focused hint

"This username appears likely to exist on GitHub."

Level 4 — Explicit guidance (only when requested)

"Search GitHub for the exact username."

This encourages learning while reducing frustration.

---

## 4. Learning Detection

The Scribe should identify moments worth preserving.

Examples:

- New investigative technique discovered.
- Better workflow identified.
- Unexpected successful pivot.
- Common mistake corrected.
- New tool evaluated.

Each should generate a recommendation to weave a new Thread into The Loom.

---

## 5. Loom Integration

After an Observation is completed, The Scribe should recommend which knowledge should become permanent.

Possible Thread types:

- Observation
- Technique
- Tool Review
- Discovery
- Pattern
- Theory
- Lesson
- Playbook Update
- Journal Entry

The Observer remains the final authority on what is woven into The Loom.

---

# Design Principles

1. Teach before telling.
2. Preserve before forgetting.
3. Encourage curiosity.
4. Distinguish facts from hypotheses.
5. Reward disciplined investigation.
6. Leave every Observer stronger than before.

---

# Success Criteria

The Scribe is successful when:

- Observations become higher quality over time.
- Investigators rely less on direct answers.
- The Loom grows with reusable knowledge instead of duplicated notes.
- Every completed investigation improves future investigations.

---

# The Watchers Principle

> The greatest achievement of an Observer is not solving today's mystery.
>
> It is ensuring the next Observer begins one step ahead.
