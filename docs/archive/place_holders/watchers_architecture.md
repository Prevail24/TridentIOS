# The Watchers Platform Architecture

Version: 1.0
Status: Foundational
Classification: Canonical

---

# Mission

The Watchers Platform exists to help people discover, preserve, organize, connect, and improve knowledge.

Applications solve problems.

The Platform preserves intelligence.

Every application within The Watchers ecosystem contributes to a shared body of knowledge known as **The Loom**.

---

# Core Philosophy

The purpose of software is not simply to produce answers.

The purpose of software is to improve the people using it.

Every investigation…

Every trade…

Every lesson…

Every discovery…

Should leave both the individual and The Loom stronger than before.

---

# The Four Pillars

## 1. The Observer

The human.

The Observer is the only entity capable of judgment.

AI may recommend.

AI may document.

AI may teach.

The Observer decides.

---

## 2. The Observatory

The interface.

The Observatory is where Observers interact with knowledge.

Responsibilities:

• Visualize Threads
• Search The Loom
• Explore relationships
• Manage investigations
• Review recommendations
• View analytics

The Observatory owns no knowledge.

It is a window into The Loom.

---

## 3. The Loom

The memory.

The Loom is the permanent knowledge layer.

Responsibilities:

• Store Threads
• Maintain relationships
• Preserve provenance
• Track revisions
• Enable discovery
• Enable learning

The Loom is application agnostic.

Applications contribute.

The Loom remembers.

---

## 4. Threads

The fundamental unit of knowledge.

Everything permanent becomes a Thread.

Examples:

Observation

Technique

Discovery

Tool

Theory

Lesson

Pattern

Journal

Strategy

Reference

Experiment

Playbook

Future thread types can be introduced without changing the architecture.

# Intelligence Roles

The platform separates responsibilities into specialized roles.

## Observer

Human investigator.

Makes decisions.

Owns final approval.

---

## Trident

Investigation specialist.

Responsible for:

• investigations
• evidence collection
• correlation
• reporting

---

## Scribe

Documentation specialist.

Responsible for:

• recording
• validation
• hint generation
• educational guidance
• quality assurance

---

## Weaver

Knowledge integration specialist.

Responsible for:

• converting completed work into Threads
• identifying duplicates
• linking related Threads
• strengthening The Loom

---

## Archivist

Knowledge retrieval specialist.

Responsible for:

• indexing
• searching
• recommendations
• relationship discovery

---

## Mentor

Learning specialist.

Responsible for:

• adapting hints
• identifying weaknesses
• recommending practice
• measuring growth

---

## Analyst

Pattern specialist.

Responsible for:

• trend analysis
• cross-domain discoveries
• anomaly detection
• intelligence summaries
